    Home and Living Spaces¶

    General Housing¶

    Korean Meaning Notes
    집 Home / House General term
    아파트 Apartment Most common in Korea
    빌라 Villa Small apartment building
    방 Room Also 방 (room)
    거실 Living room -
    부엌 Kitchen Also 주방
    화장실 Bathroom / Toilet -
    침실 Bedroom -
    현관 Entrance / Entryway -

    Examples: - 집에 가요. (I'm going home.) - 아파트에 살아요. (I live in an apartment.) - 방이 세 개 있어요. (There are three rooms.) - 화장실이 어디예요? (Where's the bathroom?)

    Locations in Context¶

    에 vs. 에서: - 에: Static location (being at) - 집에 있어요. (I'm at home.) - 에서: Action location (doing at) - 집에서 공부해요. (I study at home.)

    Location Patterns and Particles¶

    Common Patterns¶

    가다 (to go): - [place] + 에 가다 - 학교에 가요. (I go to school.)

    오다 (to come): - [place] + 에서 오다 - 집에서 왔어요. (I came from home.)

    있다 (to be at): - [place] + 에 있다 - 도서관에 있어요. (I'm at the library.)

    하다 (to do): - [place] + 에서 하다 - 공원에서 운동해요. (I exercise at the park.)

    Direction Particles¶

    • 에: to, at (destination/location)
    • 에서: from, at (origin/action location)
    • 로: toward, by way of (direction)
    • 까지: until, to (endpoint)

    Examples: - 학교에 가요. (I go to school.) - 학교에서 와요. (I come from school.) - 학교로 가요. (I go toward school.) - 학교까지 걸어가요. (I walk to/until school.)

    3. How do you say "I'm at home" in Korean?

    4. Ask where the nearest convenience store is.

    5. How do you say "Let's meet at the subway station"?

    6. Translate: "I study at a cafe."

    7. How do you say "I need to go to the hospital"?

    8. Ask how to get to the airport.

    9. Say "I'm going to the market to shop."

    10. Translate: "The bank is next to the post office."
