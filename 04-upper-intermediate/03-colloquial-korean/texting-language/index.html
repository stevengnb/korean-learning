    Texting Language¶

    Quick Reference¶

    Symbol Meaning Example
    ㅋㅋ Laughter (haha) ㅋㅋㅋ 웃겨
    ㅎㅎ Softer laugh (hehe) ㅎㅎ 그렇구나
    ㅇㅇ Yes, yeah ㅇㅇ 알았어
    ㄴㄴ No, nope ㄴㄴ 아니야
    ㅜㅜ Crying ㅜㅜ 슬퍼

    Laughter Expressions¶

    ㅋㅋㅋ (Kekeke)¶

    The standard laugh: 

    ㅋ - small chuckle
ㅋㅋ - haha
ㅋㅋㅋ - hahaha
ㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋ - HAHAHA

    Usage: 

    A: 오늘 넘어졌어 ㅠㅠ
B: ㅋㅋㅋㅋ괜찮아?

A: I fell today ㅠㅠ
B: Hahaha are you okay?

    Length indicates intensity: - More ㅋ = funnier/more laughter - Too many ㅋ (20+) = overly enthusiastic or sarcastic

    ㅎㅎ (Hehe)¶

    Softer, gentler laugh: 

    ㅎㅎ 그렇구나
Hehe I see

ㅎㅎ 고마워
Hehe thanks

    Compared to ㅋㅋ: - ㅋㅋ = haha (louder) - ㅎㅎ = hehe (softer, politer) - ㅎㅎ feels more mature/gentle

    Other Laugh Variations¶

    ㅋㄷㅋㄷ [크득크득] - Giggling sound ㅍㅎㅎ [pfft] - Snickering ㅋㅋ루ㅋㅋ - Silly/cute laugh variation ㄱㄱㄷ - 기 크들 shortened (obscure, not common)

    Yes/No Shortcuts¶

    Agreeing/Confirming¶

    ㅇㅇ [응응] - Yes, yeah 

    A: 내일 올 거지?
B: ㅇㅇ

A: You're coming tomorrow, right?
B: Yeah

    ㅇㅋ [오케이] - OK 

    ㅇㅋ 알았어
OK got it

    ㄱㄱ [고고] - Go go, let's go 

    A: 갈까?
B: ㄱㄱ

A: Shall we go?
B: Let's go

    ㅇㅈ [인정] - Agreed, I acknowledge 

    A: 이거 진짜 맛있다
B: ㅇㅈ

A: This is really delicious
B: Agreed

    Disagreeing/Denying¶

    ㄴㄴ [노노] - No, nope 

    A: 너 갈 거야?
B: ㄴㄴ 안 가

A: Are you going?
B: Nope, not going

    ㄴㄴㄴ - NO NO NO (emphasis)

    Emotional Expressions¶

    Crying/Sadness¶

    ㅠㅠ or ㅜㅜ - Crying face 

    ㅠㅠ 슬퍼
ㅠㅠ sad

시험 망했어 ㅜㅜ
I failed the exam ㅜㅜ

    Can be combined: 

    ㅠㅠㅠㅠㅠ - very sad
ㅠ_ㅠ - crying face with nose
ㅜ.ㅜ - crying face variation

    Shock/Surprise¶

    ㅎㄷㄷ [후덜덜] - Shivering from shock/fear 

    ㅎㄷㄷ 무서워
ㅎㄷㄷ scary

가격 ㅎㄷㄷ
The price ㅎㄷㄷ (shocking!)

    ㄷㄷㄷ [덜덜덜] - Trembling 

    Similar to ㅎㄷㄷ but just trembling

    Common Abbreviations¶

    Single-Character Initials¶

    ㅈㅅ [죄송] - Sorry 

    ㅈㅅ 늦었어
Sorry I'm late

    ㄱㅅ [감사] - Thanks 

    ㄱㅅ!
Thanks!

Also: ㄱㅅㄱㅅ (thank you thank you)

    ㅊㅋ [축하] - Congrats 

    ㅊㅋㅊㅋ!
Congrats!

    ㅅㄱ [수고] - Good work 

    ㅅㄱ했어!
Good work!

    ㅂㅂ [바이바이] - Bye bye 

    ㅂㅂ 내일 봐
Bye see you tomorrow

    ㅎㅇ [하이] - Hi 

    ㅎㅇ!
Hi!

    Two-Character Combinations¶

    ㄱㅊ [괜찮] - It's okay, fine 

    A: 미안해
B: ㄱㅊ아

A: Sorry
B: It's okay

    ㅇㅋ [오케이] - OK ㄴㄴ [노노] - No no ㅇㅈ [인정] - Agreed ㄱㄱ [고고] - Go go

    Full Word Abbreviations¶

    Consonant Extraction¶

    Taking first consonants from each syllable:

    ㅁㅊ [미친] - Crazy 

    ㅁㅊ 대박!
Crazy awesome!

    ㄱㅇㅇ [귀여워] - Cute 

    ㄱㅇㅇ
So cute

    ㅅㅂ [시발] - F*** (vulgar!) 

    Warning: Don't use unless you understand it's very vulgar

    ㅈㄱㄴ [전국노래자랑] - National singing contest 

    Longer phrases also abbreviated in context

    ㅁㄹ [모르] - Don't know 

    ㅁㄹ (I don't know)

    Common Phrase Abbreviations¶

    ㅇㄱㄹㅇ [이거레알] - This is real (for real) 

    ㅇㄱㄹㅇ?
Is this for real?

    ㄹㅇ [레알/리얼] - Real 

    ㄹㅇ?
Really?

ㄹㅇㅋㅋ
For real haha

    ㅈㄴ [존나] - Very (vulgar!) 

    Warning: Vulgar intensifier, avoid

    Number Substitutions¶

    Using numbers for sounds¶

    1 [일] - Can sound like "i" 2 [이] - Sounds like "i" 4 [사] - Sounds like "sa" 8 [팔] - Sounds like "pal" 10 [십] - Can mean "shit" in context

    Examples: 

    8282 [빨리빨리] - Hurry hurry
Rarely used now, mostly outdated

1004 [천사] - Angel
ㅊㅅ also used

    Emoticons (Korean Style)¶

    Face Emoticons¶

    ^^ or ^_^ - Happy, smiling 

    고마워 ^^
Thanks ^^

    ㅠㅠ or ㅜㅜ - Crying 

    슬퍼 ㅠㅠ
Sad ㅠㅠ

    ㅡㅡ - Unamused, deadpan 

    진짜? ㅡㅡ
Really? ㅡㅡ (skeptical)

    ㅎㅎ - Gentle smile ㅋㅋ - Laughing

    Combined Emoticons¶

    ^^7 - Salute
^ㅁ^ - Big smile
ㅇㅅㅇ - Surprised face
ㅇ<-< - Fish (random fun)

    ㅇㅋ - 오케이 (OK) ㄴㄴ - 노노 (no) ㄱㅊ - 괜찮 (fine, okay) ㅅㄱ - 수고 (good work) ㅈㅅ - 죄송/죄송합니다 (sorry) ㄱㅅ - 감사 (thanks) ㅊㅋ - 축하 (congrats)

    ㄱㄴㄱㄴ - 기능 기능 (function/ability - gaming) ㄷㅂㄷㅂ - 댓글 부탁해 (please comment) ㄹㅇㅋㅋ - 리얼 크크크 (really haha)

    Internet Slang Combinations¶

    ㅋㅋㄹㅃㅃ [크크르 ㅂㅂ] - Haha okay bye bye 

    Combining laugh + bye
Informal, playful

    ㅇㅈ? ㄹㅇㅋㅋ [인정? 리얼ㅋㅋ] 

    Agreed? For real haha
Stacking abbreviations

    Gaming Shortcuts¶

    ㄱㄱ [고고] - Go go (start) ㅅㄱ [수고] - Good game, good work ㄱㅈ [거짓말] - Lie, joking ㅅㅅ [ㅅㅂ soft version] - Sorry (in gaming)

    Exercise 3: 1. ✓ Very common 2. ✓ Common 3. ✓ Common 4. ✗ Too vulgar 5. ✓ Common

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Using abbreviations in formal contexts: 

    ❌ (to professor) ㄱㅅ합니다!
✓ (to professor) 감사합니다!

    2. Overusing ㅋ: 

    ㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋㅋ (30+ ㅋ)
Can seem insincere or sarcastic

    3. Mixing Korean and English abbreviations oddly: 

    ❌ LOLㅋㅋ (mixing cultures awkwardly)
✓ ㅋㅋㅋ (stick to Korean in Korean texts)
✓ LOL (in English contexts)

    Platform Differences¶

    KakaoTalk¶

    • Most common: ㅋㅋ, ㅎㅎ, ^^
    • Stickers very popular
    • Voice messages common

    Instagram/Twitter¶

    • More ㅋㅋㅋ
    • Hashtags common
    • Mix of abbreviations

    Online Games¶

    • ㄱㄱ, ㅅㄱ very common
    • Quick communication needed
    • More abbreviations

    Cultural Note¶

    Generational Differences¶

    Older generation (40+): - Prefer full words - Use ^^ more - Less comfortable with heavy abbreviation

    20s-30s: - Moderate abbreviation - ㅋㅋ, ㅎㅎ, ^^ - Mix of styles

    Teens: - Heavy abbreviation - Newest trends - Constantly evolving

    Formality in Digital Communication¶

    Even in texting, hierarchy matters:

    To friends: 

    ㅇㅇㄱㅊ ㅋㅋ
Yeah fine haha

    To seniors/professors: 

    네 괜찮습니다 ^^
Yes, I'm fine ^^

    Notice: full words + polite endings even in text

    Reading Comprehension vs. Production¶

    As a learner: 1. Understand most abbreviations (for reading) 2. Use only safe, common ones (ㅋㅋ, ㅎㅎ, ^^, ㅇㅇ) 3. Avoid vulgar or very casual ones until confident 4. Match the style of the person you're texting

    The safest approach: slightly more formal than too casual.

    Master texting language to understand Korean digital communication, but use conservatively until you're sure of appropriateness!
