## Forming Yes/No Questions

The simplest way to form questions in Korean is to add rising intonation to a statement.

### Method 1: Rising Intonation (Most Common)

Simply use the same form as a statement but with rising intonation (indicated by ?).

| Statement | Question | Translation |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| 가요. | 가요? | Do you go? |
| 먹어요. | 먹어요? | Do you eat? |
| 학생이에요. | 학생이에요? | Are you a student? |
| 한국 사람이에요. | 한국 사람이에요? | Are you Korean? |
| 재미있어요. | 재미있어요? | Is it interesting? |

### Examples in Context

1. 오늘 학교에 가요? (Are you going to school today?)
2. 커피 마셔요? (Do you drink coffee?)
3. 한국어 공부해요? (Do you study Korean?)
4. 집에 있어요? (Are you at home?)
5. 배고파요? (Are you hungry?)
6. 피곤해요? (Are you tired?)

### Method 2: -(으)ㅂ니까? (Formal)

For more formal situations, use -(으)ㅂ니까?

**Formation:**
- **Vowel stems:** Stem + -ㅂ니까?
- **Consonant stems:** Stem + -습니까?

| Verb | Stem | Question Form | Translation |
|------|------|---------------|-------------|
| 가다 | 가 | 갑니까? | Do you go? |
| 오다 | 오 | 옵니까? | Do you come? |
| 하다 | 하 | 합니까? | Do you do? |
| 먹다 | 먹 | 먹습니까? | Do you eat? |
| 읽다 | 읽 | 읽습니까? | Do you read? |
| 보다 | 보 | 봅니까? | Do you see? |

### Examples

1. 어디에 가십니까? (Where are you going? - formal/honorific)
2. 무엇을 하십니까? (What are you doing? - formal/honorific)
3. 한국어를 공부합니까? (Do you study Korean? - formal)

## Answering Yes/No Questions

### Positive Answers

**네 / 예** (Yes)
- 네 - more common in everyday conversation
- 예 - more formal

**Repeat the verb:**
1. Q: 학생이에요? (Are you a student?)
   A: 네, 학생이에요. (Yes, I'm a student.)

2. Q: 한국어 공부해요? (Do you study Korean?)
   A: 네, 공부해요. (Yes, I study it.)

### Negative Answers

**아니요** (No)

**Use negative form:**
1. Q: 학생이에요? (Are you a student?)
   A: 아니요, 학생이 아니에요. (No, I'm not a student.)

2. Q: 커피 마셔요? (Do you drink coffee?)
   A: 아니요, 안 마셔요. (No, I don't drink it.)

3. Q: 한국 사람이에요? (Are you Korean?)
   A: 아니요, 한국 사람이 아니에요. (No, I'm not Korean.)

## Question Words

### 뭐 / 무엇 (What)

**뭐** is the contracted form of **무엇**, commonly used in speech.

| Usage | Example | Translation |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| What is it? | 뭐예요? / 무엇이에요? | What is it? |
| What do you do? | 뭐 해요? / 무엇을 해요? | What do you do? |
| What do you eat? | 뭐 먹어요? | What do you eat? |
| What do you study? | 뭐 공부해요? | What do you study? |
| What is this? | 이게 뭐예요? | What is this? |
| What is that? | 그게 뭐예요? | What is that? |

**Examples:**
1. 이름이 뭐예요? (What is your name?)
2. 이게 뭐예요? (What is this?)
3. 뭐 먹을 거예요? (What will you eat?)
4. 주말에 뭐 했어요? (What did you do on the weekend?)
5. 좋아하는 음식이 뭐예요? (What food do you like?)

### 어디 (Where)

| Usage | Example | Translation |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Where is it? | 어디예요? | Where is it? |
| Where do you go? | 어디 가요? | Where are you going? |
| Where do you live? | 어디 살아요? | Where do you live? |
| Where are you from? | 어디에서 왔어요? | Where are you from? |
| Where is the bathroom? | 화장실이 어디예요? | Where is the bathroom? |

**Examples:**
1. 집이 어디예요? (Where is your home?)
2. 어디에서 공부해요? (Where do you study?)
3. 어디에서 만날까요? (Where shall we meet?)
4. 학교가 어디예요? (Where is the school?)
5. 어디 갔어요? (Where did you go?)

### 언제 (When)

| Usage | Example | Translation |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| When? | 언제예요? | When is it? |
| When do you go? | 언제 가요? | When do you go? |
| When did you come? | 언제 왔어요? | When did you come? |
| When will you do it? | 언제 할 거예요? | When will you do it? |
| When is your birthday? | 생일이 언제예요? | When is your birthday? |

**Examples:**
1. 수업이 언제예요? (When is the class?)
2. 언제 한국에 왔어요? (When did you come to Korea?)
3. 언제 만날까요? (When shall we meet?)
4. 시험이 언제예요? (When is the exam?)
5. 언제 졸업해요? (When do you graduate?)

### 왜 (Why)

| Usage | Example | Translation |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Why? | 왜요? | Why? |
| Why do you go? | 왜 가요? | Why do you go? |
| Why don't you eat? | 왜 안 먹어요? | Why don't you eat? |
| Why did you come? | 왜 왔어요? | Why did you come? |
| Why are you studying Korean? | 왜 한국어를 공부해요? | Why are you studying Korean? |

**Examples:**
1. 왜 울어요? (Why are you crying?)
2. 왜 안 왔어요? (Why didn't you come?)
3. 왜 전화 안 했어요? (Why didn't you call?)
4. 왜 그래요? (Why are you like that? / What's wrong?)
5. 왜 한국어를 배워요? (Why do you learn Korean?)

**Answers with 왜냐하면 (because):**
- 왜 한국어를 공부해요? (Why do you study Korean?)
- 왜냐하면 한국 드라마를 좋아하기 때문이에요. (Because I like Korean dramas.)

Simpler answer pattern: -아/어서 or -(으)니까
- 한국 드라마를 좋아해서요. (Because I like Korean dramas.)

## Question Patterns in Different Tenses

### Present Tense Questions

1. 지금 뭐 해요? (What are you doing now?)
2. 어디에 살아요? (Where do you live?)
3. 한국어를 공부해요? (Do you study Korean?)

### Past Tense Questions

1. 어제 뭐 했어요? (What did you do yesterday?)
2. 주말에 어디 갔어요? (Where did you go on the weekend?)
3. 점심 먹었어요? (Did you eat lunch?)

### Future Tense Questions

1. 내일 뭐 할 거예요? (What will you do tomorrow?)
2. 언제 올 거예요? (When will you come?)
3. 주말에 어디 갈 거예요? (Where will you go on the weekend?)

## Common Question Patterns

### 좋아해요? (Do you like...?)

1. 커피를 좋아해요? (Do you like coffee?)
2. 한국 음식을 좋아해요? (Do you like Korean food?)
3. 운동을 좋아해요? (Do you like exercise?)

### -았/었어요? (Did you...?)

1. 숙제 다 했어요? (Did you finish your homework?)
2. 잘 잤어요? (Did you sleep well?)
3. 재미있었어요? (Was it fun?)

### -(으)ㄹ 거예요? (Will you...?)

1. 같이 갈 거예요? (Will you go together?)
2. 이거 먹을 거예요? (Will you eat this?)
3. 내일 올 거예요? (Will you come tomorrow?)

## Tag Questions

Korean doesn't have direct equivalents to English tag questions ("right?", "isn't it?"), but these expressions serve similar purposes:

| Expression | Usage | Example |
|------------|-------|---------|
| 그렇죠? | Right? Isn't it? | 재미있죠? (It's fun, right?) |
| 그죠? | Right? (contraction) | 맛있죠? (It's delicious, right?) |
| 네? | Right? | 좋아하네? (You like it, right?) |

**Examples:**
1. 오늘 날씨 좋죠? (The weather is nice today, right?)
2. 이거 비싸죠? (This is expensive, right?)
3. 한국어 어렵죠? (Korean is difficult, right?)

## Polite Question Endings

For more polite questions, especially to strangers or elders:

| Form | Usage | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| -으세요? | Honorific question | 어디 가세요? (Where are you going?) |
| -실까요? | Honorific suggestion | 같이 가실까요? (Shall we go together?) |
| -으시겠어요? | Honorific will | 커피 드시겠어요? (Will you have coffee?) |
