# Location Particles (에 vs 에서)

!!! abstract "Quick Reference"
    **에 (static location, time, destination)**

    - Static location: 집**에** 있어요 (I'm at home)
    - Time: 3시**에** (at 3 o'clock)
    - Destination: 학교**에** 가요 (go to school)

    **에서 (action location)**

    - Action location: 도서관**에서** 공부해요 (study at library)
    - Starting point: 서울**에서** 왔어요 (came from Seoul)
    - Cannot be used with 있다/없다!

## The Key Difference

Both 에 and 에서 can mean "at" or "in" in English, but they serve different purposes:

- **에**: Static location (being/existing somewhere) OR destination
- **에서**: Dynamic location (doing an action somewhere)

## 에 (Static Location/Time/Destination)

### Use 1: Static Location (with 있다/없다)

When something or someone EXISTS or IS LOCATED somewhere, use 에:

- 저는 집**에** 있어요 (I am at home)
- 책이 책상**에** 있어요 (The book is on the desk)
- 친구가 학교**에** 없어요 (My friend is not at school)
- 고양이가 의자**에** 있어요 (The cat is on the chair)

**Key verbs that take 에**: 있다 (to exist/be), 없다 (to not exist/not be)

### Use 2: Destination (movement towards)

When you're GOING TO somewhere, use 에:

- 학교**에** 가요 (I go to school)
- 집**에** 와요 (I come home)
- 서울**에** 가요 (I go to Seoul)
- 친구 집**에** 가요 (I go to my friend's house)

**Key verbs that take 에**: 가다 (go), 오다 (come), 도착하다 (arrive), 들어가다 (enter)

### Use 3: Time

When expressing when something happens, use 에:

- 3시**에** 만나요 (Let's meet at 3 o'clock)
- 월요일**에** 일해요 (I work on Monday)
- 아침**에** 운동해요 (I exercise in the morning)
- 2020년**에** 졸업했어요 (I graduated in 2020)

**Note**: 에 is often omitted with pure time words in casual speech:
- 오늘 뭐 해요? (What are you doing today?) - no 에
- 내일 만나요 (Let's meet tomorrow) - no 에

## 에서 (Action Location)

### Use 1: Dynamic Location (doing actions)

When you DO SOMETHING at a location, use 에서:

- 도서관**에서** 공부해요 (I study at the library)
- 식당**에서** 밥을 먹어요 (I eat at a restaurant)
- 회사**에서** 일해요 (I work at the company)
- 극장**에서** 영화를 봐요 (I watch a movie at the theater)
- 공원**에서** 운동해요 (I exercise at the park)

**Common verbs with 에서**: 공부하다 (study), 일하다 (work), 먹다 (eat), 놀다 (play), 만나다 (meet), 하다 (do)

### Use 2: Starting Point (from)

When indicating where something started or came from, use 에서:

- 서울**에서** 왔어요 (I came from Seoul)
- 9시**에서** 5시까지 일해요 (I work from 9 to 5)
- 집**에서** 출발했어요 (I departed from home)

## Direct Comparison: 에 vs 에서

### Different meaning with location:

| Verb | Use 에 | Use 에서 |
|------|--------|----------|
| 있다 (be/exist) | 집**에** 있어요<br>(I'm at home - static) | ~~집**에서** 있어요~~<br>(WRONG - never use 에서 with 있다) |
| 공부하다 (study) | ~~학교**에** 공부해요~~<br>(WRONG - studying is an action) | 학교**에서** 공부해요<br>(I study at school - action) |
| 가다 (go) | 학교**에** 가요<br>(I go to school - destination) | ~~학교**에서** 가요~~<br>(WRONG - unless meaning "from") |

### Same location, different particles:

- 도서관**에** 있어요 (I am at the library - just being there)
- 도서관**에서** 공부해요 (I study at the library - doing action there)

- 집**에** 가요 (I go home - destination)
- 집**에** 있어요 (I'm at home - static location)
- 집**에서** 쉬어요 (I rest at home - action)

- 카페**에** 있어요 (I'm at the cafe - just being there)
- 카페**에서** 커피를 마셔요 (I drink coffee at the cafe - action)

## Special Cases and Exceptions

### Meeting people: 에서 (usually)

Even though you might think meeting is "at" a location, use 에서:

- 친구를 카페**에서** 만나요 (I meet my friend at the cafe)
- 명동**에서** 만나요 (Let's meet in Myeongdong)

But if just arriving at a location: 명동**에** 가요

### Living/residing: 에

Even though living somewhere is long-term, use 에:

- 서울**에** 살아요 (I live in Seoul)
- 한국**에** 살아요 (I live in Korea)

Think of it as existing in that location.

### Taking a class/course: 에서

- 학교**에서** 한국어를 배워요 (I learn Korean at school)
- 대학교**에서** 공부해요 (I study at university)

But: 대학교**에** 다녀요 (I attend university)

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using 에서 with 있다/없다
This is the most common error!

- Wrong: 집에서 있어요
- Right: 집에 있어요

**Rule**: ALWAYS use 에 with 있다/없다, NEVER 에서!

### Mistake 2: Using 에 for action locations
- Wrong: 도서관에 공부해요
- Right: 도서관에서 공부해요

If you're DOING something actively, use 에서.

### Mistake 3: Using 에서 for destinations
- Wrong: 학교에서 가요 (unless you mean "from school")
- Right: 학교에 가요 (going TO school)

Movement TOWARDS = 에
Movement FROM = 에서

### Mistake 4: Confusing time and location
- 3시에 만나요 (Let's meet at 3 o'clock) - time uses 에
- 카페에서 만나요 (Let's meet at the cafe) - action location uses 에서
