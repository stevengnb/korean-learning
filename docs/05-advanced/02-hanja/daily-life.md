# Hanja in Daily Life

## Where You'll See Hanja

### 1. Signs & Public Spaces

**Exit/Entrance signs:**
- 出口 (출구) - Exit
- 入口 (입구) - Entrance
- 非常口 (비상구) - Emergency Exit

**Buildings:**
- 圖書館 (도서관) - Library
- 美術館 (미술관) - Art Museum
- 博物館 (박물관) - Museum
- 百貨店 (백화점) - Department Store

### 2. Newspapers

**Headlines sometimes show hanja in parentheses:**
```
대통령(大統領) 방문
수출(輸出) 증가(增加)
교육(敎育) 개혁(改革)
```

**Why?** Clarifies meaning for important/ambiguous terms.

### 3. Names

**Personal names (이름):**
Almost all Korean names use hanja:
- 김민준 (金民俊)
- 이서연 (李瑞妍)
- 박지호 (朴智浩)

**Place names:**
- 서울 (漢城/首爾)
- 부산 (釜山)
- 대구 (大邱)

### 4. Formal Documents

**Legal documents:**
- 契約書 (계약서) - Contract
- 證明書 (증명서) - Certificate
- 申請書 (신청서) - Application

**Business cards (명함):**
Often show hanja for names and titles:
- 社長 (사장) - Company President
- 部長 (부장) - Department Head

### 5. Academic Contexts

**Textbooks:**
- 數學 (수학) - Mathematics
- 科學 (과학) - Science
- 歷史 (역사) - History

**Degrees:**
- 學士 (학사) - Bachelor's
- 碩士 (석사) - Master's
- 博士 (박사) - Doctorate

## Common Daily Vocabulary

### Time & Calendar

**Days of week (all use hanja!):**
- 月曜日 (월요일) - Monday (Moon day)
- 火曜日 (화요일) - Tuesday (Fire day)
- 水曜日 (수요일) - Wednesday (Water day)
- 木曜日 (목요일) - Thursday (Wood day)
- 金曜日 (금요일) - Friday (Metal day)
- 土曜日 (토요일) - Saturday (Earth day)
- 日曜日 (일요일) - Sunday (Sun day)

**Months:**
- 一月 (일월) - January

## Cultural Expressions

### Four-Character Idioms (四字成語)

**일석이조 (一石二鳥)**
- One stone, two birds
- Kill two birds with one stone

**자화자찬 (自畫自讚)**
- Self drawing, self praise
- Blowing your own horn

**동문서답 (東問西答)**
- East question, west answer
- Irrelevant answer

### Traditional Concepts

**오륜 (五倫)** - Five Moral Principles
**삼강 (三綱)** - Three Fundamental Principles
**효도 (孝道)** - Filial piety

## Recognition vs. Production

**You need to:**
- ✓ Recognize common hanja
- ✓ Understand hanja-based words
- ✓ Read signs with hanja

**You don't need to:**
- ✗ Write hanja by hand
- ✗ Memorize every character
- ✗ Use hanja in daily writing (Hangul is standard)

## Practical Tips

**When you see unfamiliar Sino-Korean word:**

1. Look for familiar hanja components
2. Break down into parts
3. Deduce approximate meaning
4. Verify with dictionary if needed

**Example: 도서관**
- 圖 (picture/plan) - might not know
- 書 (book) - you know this!
- 館 (building) - you know this!
- → Probably related to books and buildings → Library!

## Bottom Line

Hanja is everywhere in Korean life, even if not always visible. Understanding common characters:
- Makes vocabulary learning easier
- Helps read formal texts
- Clarifies confusing words
- Deepens cultural understanding

Focus on the most common 100-300 characters for practical daily use!
