# Business Korean

## Essential Business Vocabulary

### Company Structure
- 회사 (會社) - company
- 본사 (本社) - headquarters
- 지사 (支社) - branch office
- 부서 (部署) - department
- 팀 (team) - team

### Positions & Titles
- 사장 (社長) - president/CEO
- 부사장 - vice president
- 전무 - executive director
- 상무 - managing director
- 이사 (理事) - director
- 부장 (部長) - department head
- 과장 (課長) - section chief
- 대리 - assistant manager
- 사원 (社員) - employee

### Business Activities
- 회의 (會議) - meeting
- 출장 (出張) - business trip
- 보고 (報告) - report
- 계약 (契約) - contract
- 협상 (協商) - negotiation
- 제안 (提案) - proposal
- 프로젝트 - project
- 마감 - deadline

## Common Business Expressions

### Meetings
```
회의를 시작하겠습니다.
Let's begin the meeting.

안건을 말씀드리겠습니다.
I'll present the agenda.

의견을 말씀해 주십시오.
Please share your opinion.

검토해 보겠습니다.
We'll review it.
```

### Email Phrases
```
메일 확인하셨습니까?
Did you check the email?

첨부 파일을 참고해 주십시오.
Please refer to the attached file.

회신 부탁드립니다.
Please reply.

확인 후 연락드리겠습니다.
I'll contact you after checking.
```

### Phone Calls
```
○○ 회사 △△입니다.
This is △△ from ○○ Company.

담당자를 바꿔 드리겠습니다.
I'll transfer you to the person in charge.

메시지를 남기시겠습니까?
Would you like to leave a message?

다시 전화드리겠습니다.
I'll call back.
```

### Requests & Responses
```
부탁드립니다.
I ask for your help.

알겠습니다.
Understood.

검토하겠습니다.
I'll review it.

확인 후 답변드리겠습니다.
I'll respond after checking.
```

## Business Meeting Structure

**Opening:**
```
바쁘신 중에 참석해 주셔서 감사합니다.
Thank you for attending despite your busy schedule.
```

**Presenting:**
```
먼저 ~에 대해 말씀드리겠습니다.
First, I'll speak about ~.

다음 슬라이드를 보시겠습니다.
Please look at the next slide.
```

**Q&A:**
```
질문 있으십니까?
Do you have any questions?

좋은 질문입니다.
That's a good question.
```

**Closing:**
```
오늘 회의는 여기까지 하겠습니다.
That concludes today's meeting.
```

## Negotiation Language

```
조건을 검토해 보겠습니다.
We'll review the conditions.

할인이 가능하신가요?
Is a discount possible?

재고하겠습니다.
We'll reconsider.

합의점을 찾아봅시다.
Let's find common ground.
```

## Cultural Notes

**Hierarchy matters:**
- Always use proper titles
- Defer to seniors in meetings
- Business cards (명함) exchanged formally

**Communication style:**
- Indirect, not blunt
- Use humble language
- Show respect through formality

This is essential for professional Korean business environments!
